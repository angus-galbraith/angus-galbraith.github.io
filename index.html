Angus's Blog

Discovery

The 5th of January 2021, started off the same as nearly any other day for me. When the alarm went off Emma and I headed downstairs and began our usual morning routine. I as always head into the kitchen, oven on i feed the cats then put the bacon in the oven. It is not unusual at this time for me to pick up my darts and have a couple of throws, on the this occasion it was almost a life saver. My third dart had gone quite high and as I stretched my left arm to reach it, I felt a very slight tug just inside my collarbone. Immediately I felt the area ddiscovering a small oval lump approximately 2cms long. At 9am that day I made contact with my GP through the askmyGP app that was being used due to covid.

Investigations

My first face to face with a Doctor was the same day. Following an examination of the lump he requested bloods and ultrasound scans. The plan had been for a visting radioligists to scan and take a needle aspiration biopsy, they were due in Shetland on the 25th of January and and appointment was made for me. As is often the case with Island life the weather closed in and no planes arrived that day. Soon after I met with one of the local surgeons who immediately requested a CT scan and made a referral to Aberdeen Royals ENT dept for biopsy and follow up.

Diagnoses

I had a phone call after the needle biopsy, which informed me it looked like I had Diffuse Large B Cell Lymphoma . A core biopsy was arranged to verify.

Treatment

My treatment was due to start on Tuesdsay 1st of June, but while travelling to Aberdeen by boat the orevious night my left arm became very swollen.
